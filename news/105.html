Fairfax FLAGS Update: Raise Your Voice and Rally for FCPS Funding!

by Fairfax FLAGS

Fairfax FLAGS Update: Raise Your Voice and Rally for FCPS Funding!

On Jan. 7, FCPS Superintendent Dr. Karen Garza proposed a fully funded (“no cuts”) budget, but the language immersion programs remain at risk because FCPS faces a deficit up to $75 million.

The School Board is reviewing the proposed budget, and the Board of Supervisors and state legislators are considering how much money to transfer to FCPS.

Your elected officials need to hear your voice today -- without your support, FCPS may not receive sufficient funding, putting programs like language immersion at risk for elimination.

  1. SEND AN EMAIL TODAY! Click here for form letters and email addresses for the School Board, the Board of Supervisors, and State Legislators. In less than 5 minutes, you can influence your elected officials to fully fund our schools.
  2. ATTEND OUR “INVEST IN KIDS” RALLY ON TUESDAY, FEB. 16! Please join us to advocate for full funding for our schools during an indoor rally on Tuesday, Feb. 16, from 4:45-5:45 pm at the indoor amphitheater of the Fairfax County Government Center (12000 Government Center Pkwy, Fairfax).

    We need all parents, students, and community members to come out and make a strong and visible show of support, so the Board of Supervisors provides the necessary funding for FCPS.

    The Board of Supervisors contributes 70% of the FCPS budget, so their decision on funding will determine the future of our school system and programs like language immersion.

    The rally is sponsored by Fairfax FLAGS, #IamFCPS, Save Fairfax Band and Strings (FACE), Fairfax Education Association, and Fairfax County Council of PTAs.

  3. JOIN FLAGS! Sign up for our mailing list at fairfaxflags.org and “like” our page at facebook.com/fairfaxflags in order to receive periodic updates and advocacy guidance.
  4. SPREAD THE WORD! Please share this email with anyone who is interested in preserving FCPS world language programs and sustaining the quality of our schools.
